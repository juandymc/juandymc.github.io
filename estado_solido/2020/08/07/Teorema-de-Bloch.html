Teorema de Bloch

Aug 7, 2020

Theorem. Los autoestados $\psi$ del hamiltoniano de un electrón $H=-\hbar^{2} \nabla^{2} / 2 m+$ $U(\mathbf{r}),$ donde $U(\mathbf{r}+\mathbf{R})=U(\mathbf{r})$ para todo en una red de Bravais se puede escoger de modo que sea equivalente a

\[\begin{equation*}\psi_{n \mathbf{k}}(\mathbf{r})=e^{\mathbf{i k} \cdot \mathbf{r}} u_{n \mathbf{k}}(\mathbf{r})\end{equation*}\]

donde

\[\begin{equation*}u_{n k}(\mathbf{r}+\mathbf{R})=u_{n \mathbf{k}}(\mathbf{r})\end{equation*}\]

para todo $\mathbf{R}$ en la red de Bravais. Una consecuencia inmediata sería

\[\begin{equation*}\psi_{n \mathrm{k}}(\mathbf{r}+\mathbf{R})=e^{i \mathbf{k} \cdot \mathbf{R}} \psi_{n \mathbf{k}}(\mathbf{r})\end{equation*}\] \[\begin{equation*}T_{\mathrm{R}} f(\mathbf{r})=f(\mathbf{r}+\mathbf{R})\end{equation*}\]

dado que el hamiltoniano es periódico

\[\begin{equation*}T_{\mathrm{R}} H \psi=H(\mathbf{r}+\mathbf{R}) \psi(\mathbf{r}+\mathbf{R})=H(\mathbf{r}) \psi(\mathbf{r}+\mathbf{R})=H T_{\mathrm{R}} \psi\end{equation*}\]

de modo que

\[\begin{equation*}T_{\mathrm{R}} H=H T_{\mathrm{R}}\end{equation*}\]

el hamiltoniano y el operador traslación conmutan. Podemos elegir una base de autoestados comunes a ambos operadores

\[\begin{equation*}\begin{array}{l} H \psi=\varepsilon \psi \\ T_{\mathrm{R}} \psi=c(\mathbf{R})\psi \end{array}\end{equation*}\] \[\begin{equation*}T_{\mathrm{R}} T_{\mathrm{R}} \psi(\mathbf{r})=T_{\mathrm{R}} \cdot T_{\mathrm{R}} \psi(\mathbf{r})=\psi\left(\mathbf{r}+\mathbf{R}+\mathbf{R}^{\prime}\right)\end{equation*}\]

por lo tanto

\[\begin{equation*}T_{\mathbf{R}} T_{\mathbf{R}^{\prime}}=T_{\mathbf{R}^{\prime}} T_{\mathbf{R}}=T_{\mathbf{R}+\mathbf{R}^{\prime}}\end{equation*}\] \[\begin{equation*}T_{\mathrm{R}} T_{\mathrm{R}} \psi=c(\mathbf{R}) T_{\mathrm{R}} \psi=c(\mathbf{R}) c\left(\mathbf{R}^{\prime}\right) \psi\end{equation*}\] \[\begin{equation*}T_{\mathbf{R}^\prime} T_{\mathbf{R}} \psi=T_{\mathbf{R}+\mathbf{R}^\prime} \psi=c\left(\mathbf{R}+\mathbf{R}^{\prime}\right) \psi\end{equation*}\] \[\begin{equation*}c\left(\mathbf{R}+\mathbf{R}^{\prime}\right)=c(\mathbf{R}) c\left(\mathbf{R}^{\prime}\right)\end{equation*}\] \[\begin{equation*}c\left(\mathbf{a}_{i}\right)=e^{2 \pi i x_{i}}\end{equation*}\] \[\begin{equation*}\mathbf{R}=n_{1} \mathbf{a}_{1}+n_{2} \mathbf{a}_{2}+n_{3} \mathbf{a}_{3}\end{equation*}\] \[\begin{equation*}c(\mathbf{R})=c\left(\mathbf{a}_{1}\right)^{n_{1}} c\left(\mathbf{a}_{2}\right)^{n_2} c\left(\mathbf{a}_{3}\right)^{n_{3}}\end{equation*}\] \[\begin{equation*}c(\mathbf{R})=e^{i \mathbf{k} \cdot \mathbf{R}}\end{equation*}\]

donde

\[\begin{equation*}\mathbf{k}=x_{1} \mathbf{b}_{1}+x_{2} \mathbf{b}_{2}+x_{3} \mathbf{b}_{3}\end{equation*}\]

dado que $\mathbf{b}{i} \cdot \mathbf{a}{j}=2 \pi \delta_{i j}$. Si tomamos un autoestado del hamiltoniano $\psi$ se debe cumplir [1, p. 135]

\[\begin{equation*}T_{\mathrm{R}} \psi=\psi(\mathbf{r}+\mathbf{R})=c(\mathbf{R}) \psi=e^{\mathbf{i} \mathbf{k} \cdot \mathbf{R}} \psi(\mathbf{r})\end{equation*}\]

References
